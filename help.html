🚀 Getting Started

What is Live Transcript?
Live Transcript is a Google Meet add-on that provides real-time speech-to-text transcription during your meetings. It captures audio from participants and converts it to text with speaker identification.
How does Live Transcript work?
Live Transcript uses advanced AI technology to process audio streams from Google Meet in real-time. It identifies speakers and converts their speech to text, displaying the results in an easy-to-read format.
What do I need to use Live Transcript?
You need a Google Workspace account with Google Meet access, a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge), microphone permissions, and a stable internet connection.

📥 Installation & Setup

How do I install Live Transcript?
Go to the Google Workspace Marketplace, search for "Live Transcript," and click Install. Follow the prompts to grant necessary permissions. See our Setup Guide for detailed instructions.
Why can't I see the Live Transcript icon in Google Meet?
Make sure the add-on is properly installed and you've granted all necessary permissions. Refresh your browser page and check that you're using a supported browser with microphone access enabled.
What permissions does Live Transcript need?
Live Transcript needs access to Google Meet sessions, audio streams, and participant information. These permissions are required for real-time transcription functionality.
Can I install Live Transcript for my entire organization?
Yes, Google Workspace administrators can install Live Transcript for their organization. See our Admin Configuration Guide for details.

🎤 Using Transcription

How do I start transcription in a meeting?
Click the Live Transcript icon in Google Meet, then click "Start Activity" to open the main transcript view. Finally, click "Start Live Transcript" to begin real-time transcription.
Can I pause or stop transcription?
Yes, you can pause or stop transcription at any time using the controls in the Live Transcript panel. The transcription will stop immediately, and you can restart it when needed.
How accurate is the transcription?
Transcription accuracy depends on audio quality, speaker clarity, and background noise. In good conditions, Live Transcript typically achieves 90%+ accuracy using advanced AI technology.
Can I see who is speaking?
Yes, Live Transcript identifies speakers and displays their names with each transcript line. This helps you follow conversations and know who said what.
Can I copy or save the transcript?
Yes, you can copy transcript text and save it for future reference. The transcript is displayed in real-time and can be copied using standard browser copy functions.

💡 Pro Tip

For best transcription results, ensure participants speak clearly, minimize background noise, and use a good quality microphone. The better the audio input, the more accurate the transcription will be.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Why isn't my microphone being detected?
Check your browser's microphone permissions. Go to your browser settings and ensure microphone access is allowed for Google Meet. Also, make sure your microphone isn't muted or disabled.
Transcription is not starting - what should I do?
First, check your internet connection. Then verify that microphone permissions are granted. Try refreshing the page and restarting the transcription. If the issue persists, contact support.
The transcript text is garbled or inaccurate
Poor audio quality often causes transcription issues. Check for background noise, ensure speakers are close to their microphones, and verify that audio levels are appropriate. Clear speech produces better results.
Live Transcript is running slowly or lagging
This is usually due to internet connection issues or browser performance. Try closing other browser tabs, check your internet speed, and ensure you're using a supported browser version.
I'm getting error messages - what do they mean?
Error messages usually indicate permission issues, connectivity problems, or browser compatibility. Check our Support page for specific error solutions, or contact support with the exact error message.

🛡️ Privacy & Security

Is my audio data secure?
Yes, your audio is processed in real-time and is not permanently stored. All data transmission is encrypted, and we follow strict privacy guidelines. See our Privacy Policy for details.
Where is my audio data processed?
Audio is processed in real-time using secure, encrypted connections. The data is not stored on our servers and is handled according to our privacy policy and industry standards.
Can other meeting participants see the transcript?
Only users who have installed and activated Live Transcript can see the transcript. It's not automatically shared with other participants unless you choose to share it.
Do I need to inform participants about transcription?
Yes, it's recommended to inform all meeting participants that transcription is active. This promotes transparency and ensures everyone is aware of the recording/transcription.

⚠️ Privacy Reminder

Always inform meeting participants when transcription is active. Respect privacy preferences and be mindful of sensitive information that might be discussed.

⚙️ Technical Questions

What browsers are supported?
Live Transcript works with Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions). We recommend using the most recent browser version for the best experience.
Does Live Transcript work on mobile devices?
Live Transcript is designed for desktop browsers. Mobile support may be limited due to browser restrictions and audio processing requirements.
What internet speed do I need?
A stable internet connection with at least 1 Mbps upload speed is recommended for optimal transcription performance. Higher speeds provide better real-time processing.
Can I use Live Transcript in large meetings?
Yes, Live Transcript can handle meetings with multiple participants. However, very large meetings (50+ participants) may experience reduced performance depending on audio quality and network conditions.
Does Live Transcript work with Google Meet's recording feature?
Live Transcript works independently of Google Meet's recording feature. You can use both simultaneously, but they serve different purposes - Live Transcript provides real-time text, while recording captures video/audio.

🌐 Browser-Specific Issues

Chrome: How do I enable microphone permissions?
Click the lock icon in the address bar, ensure "Microphone" is set to "Allow," and refresh the page if needed. You can also check Chrome's site settings for Google Meet.
Firefox: Microphone not working
Click the shield icon in the address bar, go to "Permissions," and ensure microphone access is allowed. Check Firefox's privacy settings and refresh the page.
Safari: Permission issues
Go to Safari > Preferences > Websites, select "Microphone," and ensure it's set to "Allow" for Google Meet. You may need to restart Safari after changing permissions.
Edge: Audio access problems
Click the lock icon in the address bar, check microphone permissions, and ensure Edge has access to your microphone. Update to the latest version if issues persist.
